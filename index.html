An invitation

Six years, two cities, one very persistent dog. Charlie and James are finally doing the thing — and they want you in the Hunter for the weekend to do it with them. Come Friday, stay till Sunday, leave with sore feet and a slight headache. That's the brief.

Their story

How it began

Charlie says she met James at a friend's housewarming in Newtown. James says they actually met three months earlier at the same friend's birthday, and Charlie has no memory of it. They've never resolved this.

What's not in dispute: by the second meeting, Charlie had decided. By the fourth, James had too. Six years, two flat moves, one rescue greyhound (Mabel, who will not be at the wedding, and who is taking it personally), and a proposal on the headland at South Coogee one impossibly clear morning in March.

They picked the Hunter because it's where Charlie's grandparents got married in 1962, and because James thinks the Semillon here is the best wine in the country. He is correct about this.

"He proposed before I'd had coffee.
Reader, I still said yes."

Getting here

The road to Pokolbin

◎

By car from Sydney

About 2.5 hours up the M1. Plenty of parking at Squire's. Set off Friday lunchtime to beat the afternoon traffic out of the city.

◇

By air

Newcastle Airport (NTL) is 45 minutes from Pokolbin. Sydney (SYD) for international guests — hire a car on arrival and drive up.

△

By train

NSW TrainLink from Sydney Central to Newcastle Interchange, then 45 minutes by taxi. Slower but a beautiful ride along the Hawkesbury.

○

Saturday shuttle

Running from Crowne Plaza Hunter Valley to Squire's, returning at 9:30pm and 11pm.
